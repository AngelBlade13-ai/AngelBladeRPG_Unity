# Player Promise And Feature Hierarchy

## Player Promise

**Petals in the Dusk is a story-driven fantasy RPG where characters and their
relationships are the main draw, and exploration, jobs, and turn-based combat
deepen the player's connection to the party and their changing world.**

Systems support attachment, expression, and story pacing. They should not bury
the party under complexity or displace the emotional reason to continue.

## Tier 1: Protected

These features define the game and survive ordinary scope pressure:

- Main story clarity, pacing, and emotional payoff.
- Companion writing and distinct character voices.
- Found-family banter and party chemistry.
- The world's gradual decay and melancholy atmosphere.
- Responsive, readable core turn-based combat.

A cut or simplification that materially weakens Tier 1 requires an explicit
product-direction decision rather than an ordinary production compromise.

## Tier 2: Strong Support

These features strongly support the promise but may become narrower if the
demo budget requires it:

- Job and affinity depth beyond the minimum viable strategic choices.
- Ember Camp banter and optional companion moments.
- Environmental storytelling beyond critical-path readability.
- Side quests that deepen characters, locations, and local concerns.

Tier 2 should be simplified by reducing quantity before reducing coherence.

## Tier 3: Nice To Have

These features may be reduced or deferred without breaking the core promise:

- Full-game roster size beyond the demo cast.
- Elaborate itemization and large loot quantities.
- Deep speedrun routing and exploration-reward layers.
- Broad protagonist appearance customization.

## Scope Test

When adding or cutting a feature, ask:

1. Which player-promise phrase does it support?
2. Which tier owns it?
3. Does it strengthen character connection or merely add quantity?
4. Can an existing system deliver the same value more clearly?
5. What art, writing, code, testing, and save-data obligations does it create?
