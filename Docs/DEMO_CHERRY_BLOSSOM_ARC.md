# Demo Cherry Blossom Arc And Ending

This document defines the transition from the Grassland Goblin Boss through the
Cherry Blossom regional boss and public-demo ending. It complements
`DEMO_STORY_REFERENCE.md` and does not reveal the full game's explanation for
the world's later problems.

## Arc Sequence

```text
Goblin Boss defeated
  -> warm return to Suncrest Hollow
  -> party respite at the Suncrest Inn
  -> overdue Cherry Blossom caravan request
  -> road travel and Ember Camp introduction
  -> nomadic settlement and sacred-tree unease
  -> territorial Great Stag encounter
  -> unresolved scene at the tree
  -> environmental progression block and demo ending
```

## Return To Suncrest Hollow

After defeating the Goblin Boss, the party returns to a genuinely warm welcome.
Suncrest Hollow is small enough that news has arrived before them.

- Captain Vashti formally closes the goblin-threat quest thread.
- The town recognizes the party for clearing the road. The exact ceremonial
  title and small material reward remain to be chosen.
- Old Marlow may briefly thank the party again and help bridge into the next
  request.
- A short Suncrest Inn scene gives the full party breathing room before the
  tone changes.

The inn scene is character-focused rather than plot-heavy. It may call back to
the tutorial battle, let Damari and Enora show their established dynamic, and
show Lysander still finding his place in the group.

## Reason To Enter Cherry Blossom

A trade caravan from Cherry Blossom's nomadic settlement is overdue. The delay
is unusual because the caravan is normally reliable, but it is not initially
treated as a crisis.

- Old Marlow or Captain Vashti asks the newly trusted party to check the road
  and make contact. The final quest-giver choice remains open.
- The premise intentionally mirrors the delayed-caravan opening: the same kind
  of grounded task now comes to a capable, recognized party rather than an
  unknown newcomer.
- This request provides the immediate reason to travel through the newly opened
  route instead of ending the Grassland arc with another routine town return.

## Ember Camp Introduction

Ember Camp appears for the first time on the road to Cherry Blossom or shortly
after arrival. The exact access point remains a map-layout decision.

- This is the party's first extended trip away from Suncrest Hollow.
- The demo camp contains one or two companion conversations.
- One conversation may feature Damari and Enora's marriage-like running banter
  without confirming relationship details that have not been authored.
- Another may let Lysander explain a little of why he is choosing to remain with
  this party, without turning into a full backstory reveal.
- Camp remains a recovery and companion space, not a replacement town service
  center.

## Nomadic Settlement Problem

A significant sacred tree or grove stands at the center of the settlement's
relationship with the land and seasons. It has begun blooming and shedding at
the wrong times and at an unnatural speed, as though its internal seasonal
rhythm is no longer reliable.

- The tree remains visually alive and beautiful. It shows no rot, corruption,
  or explicit supernatural source.
- The settlement does not know why this is happening.
- The tree's exact name and the precise tradition connecting it to the nomads'
  seasonal movement remain to be authored.

This gives Cherry Blossom one concrete image of unease without explaining the
larger story.

## Environmental Unease

- Elders perform additional offerings or rituals at the sacred tree but avoid
  claiming they understand the problem.
- Livestock and wildlife act skittish and avoid places where they normally
  graze.
- Petals fall in completely still air without an NPC calling attention to it.
- One settlement leader or elder will admit, when directly approached, that
  something feels wrong but that they cannot name it.

No NPC knows the true cause. Dialogue must preserve honest uncertainty and
cannot expose later lore.

## Regional Boss: The Great Stag

The boss is **The Great Stag**, a large territorial animal remembered in local
folklore as protective of the sacred tree and surrounding land rather than
naturally hostile.

The tree's disturbance has agitated or distressed the beast. It now attacks
anyone approaching its territory, including the party. It is a suffering
protector, not a villain and not a source of the region's problem.

Its branch-like flowering antlers symbolize seasons and renewal. The party
subdues rather than kills it through a two-phase solo battle built around a
marked charge, Defend and taunt responses, party-wide pressure, and a low-HP
Panicked state. The authoritative combat and outcome rules are recorded in
`CHERRY_BLOSSOM_COMBAT_DESIGN.md`.

## Demo Ending

Subduing the Great Stag makes the immediate area safe and allows the creature to
calm, but it does not restore the sacred tree.

- The party and settlement receive no definitive explanation.
- A quiet scene at the tree replaces a clean victory celebration.
- The settlement elder offers a short admission of uncertainty.
- Party reactions should remain in character: Damari may deflect with dark
  humor, while Lysander treats the implications seriously.
- The final emotional note is unresolved and slightly heavy, marking the point
  where the grounded adventure begins to reveal a deeper problem.

An environmental obstruction blocks travel beyond the demo, such as a
landslide or a still-dangerous route. Its exact form is chosen with the map
layout. The ending should transition to a clear demo-complete screen rather
than presenting unfinished content as accessible.
