## Rights And Ownership

The final reviewed agreement should address all of the following explicitly:

- Whether any qualifying contribution is intended as work made for hire.
- A signed copyright assignment to Owner for accepted deliverables, including
  an assignment fallback where work-made-for-hire treatment does not apply.
- Rights to reproduce, modify, adapt, distribute, publicly perform/display,
  market, port, localize, sublicense, and otherwise use accepted deliverables
  as part of or in connection with the Project.
- Delivery of editable source files and materials needed to exercise the rights.
- Waiver or non-assertion of moral rights only where legally permitted.
- Contributor's retained portfolio-display rights, timing, and confidentiality
  restrictions.
- Treatment of rejected work, pre-existing materials, tools, fonts, samples,
  plugins, and third-party content.

Do not assume that calling commissioned work `work made for hire` is sufficient.
U.S. commissioned-work categories are limited, and copyright transfers generally
require a signed writing. The final agreement should be reviewed accordingly.

## Contributor Promises

The reviewed agreement should require the Contributor to confirm that:

- Their contribution is original except for disclosed, approved material.
- They have authority to grant the stated rights.
- The deliverable does not knowingly infringe another party's copyright,
  trademark, privacy, publicity, contract, or other rights.
- Every third-party asset, font, sample, brush, plugin, model, and reference with
  usage restrictions is disclosed in writing before delivery.
- Generative-AI use, model/service, inputs, outputs, and applicable terms are
  disclosed and approved before inclusion.
- No conflicting license, lien, security interest, or prior assignment applies.
- They will reasonably assist in documenting ownership or correcting a rights
  issue associated with their contribution.

## Working Relationship

The reviewed agreement should define independent-contractor or other status,
control of schedule and methods, equipment, taxes, expenses, repository access,
security, confidentiality, publicity, non-exclusivity, and authority to bind the
Project. Labels alone do not override applicable employment law.

## Termination And Project Changes

Define termination rights, notice, payment for accepted work, delivery of
work-in-progress, rights in paid/accepted deliverables, return or deletion of
confidential material, cancellation, Project renaming, delayed release, and the
possibility that accepted work may be modified or not used.

A Contributor may step back from future work under the reviewed agreement's
notice terms without erasing rights already granted or compensation already
owed for accepted work.

## General Terms

The final agreement should include governing law and venue, notices, assignment,
entire agreement, amendments in signed writing, severability, waiver, electronic
signatures/counterparts, and survival of ownership, payment, confidentiality,
warranties, and dispute terms.
