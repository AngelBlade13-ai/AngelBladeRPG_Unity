# Product Identity

## Public Identity

- Game title: **Petals in the Dusk**.
- Working developer brand: **Rawr! Studios**.
- Working publisher brand: **Rawr! Studios**.
- Repository/internal project ID: `AngelBladeRPG_Unity`.

The internal ID may remain in assembly names, namespaces, prototype editor
menus, and repository URLs. Player-facing builds, store materials, credits, and
marketing use the public title.

## Steamworks Account Plan

The planned onboarding form is individual/sole proprietor. Enter the owner's
full legal name as Steamworks' legal company name and ensure it matches bank and
tax records. Do not enter `Rawr! Studios` as the legal onboarding name unless it
later becomes the matching legal entity.

Steam store pages separately accept developer and publisher display names and
can link them to a controlled Creator Homepage. The intended display brand is
`Rawr! Studios` after clearance.

A sole proprietorship is not a separate legal entity and does not separate
personal and business liabilities. State or local trade-name, assumed-name, or
DBA requirements may still apply to public use of a brand. Confirm the rules in
the owner's jurisdiction before commerce begins.

## Preliminary Name Screen

This is an obvious-conflict screen, not legal clearance or a trademark opinion.

- No obvious exact video-game or Steam result for `Petals in the Dusk` was
  found in the July 16, 2026 web screen.
- Similar `RAWR! Studio`, `Rawr Studio`, and `Rawr Studios` brands already exist
  in entertainment art, design, games, and media.
- The punctuation and pluralization in `Rawr! Studios` do not eliminate likely
  search, handle, attribution, or audience-confusion risk.

Examples observed during the preliminary screen:

- `https://www.rawr.pt/`
- `https://raawrstudio.itch.io/`
- `https://www.rawr-studio.it/en/about`

Before a Coming Soon page, public logo commission, domain purchase, or social
campaign:

1. Decide whether to retain or replace `Rawr! Studios` after reviewing the
   known similar uses.
2. Search the relevant trademark databases and game/storefront catalogs in the
   intended sales territories.
3. Check domains, social handles, Steam Creator names, and support-email
   availability.
4. Obtain qualified legal advice if the brand will carry significant expense
   or long-term commercial value.
5. Record the final decision and evidence in this document.
